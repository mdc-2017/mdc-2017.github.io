The Modeling Developmental Change Workshop 2017 is an official satellite event of the Flux Congress.

Longitudinal MRI datasets require special consideration for processing and analysis, yet the field as a whole has not standardized best practices for these datasets, which could be one reason why it is difficult to replicate results across laboratories and research studies. This two-day workshop will teach best practices for processing, analyzing, modeling, and interpreting longitudinal neuroimaging data. This will help researchers conduct robust and reproducible research on how the brain and cognition change across development.

Dates: September 14-15, 2017

Costs: Free to attend, but no refreshments provided.

Location: Portland, OR, USA

Participants are encouraged to download Docker. We will share a link to the tutorial's docker environment closer to the event date.
